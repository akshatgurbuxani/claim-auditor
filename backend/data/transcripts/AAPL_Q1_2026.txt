Apple Inc. Q1 FY2026 Earnings Call Transcript
Date: January 30, 2026

Tim Cook, CEO:

Good afternoon, everyone. Thank you for joining us today. I'm pleased to report an outstanding first quarter for fiscal year 2026. Revenue for the quarter came in at $143.8 billion, a record for any quarter in Apple's history. This represents approximately 16% growth year over year compared to last year's holiday quarter revenue of $124.3 billion.

Our gross margin was 48.2%, reflecting continued strength in our product mix and Services business. Gross profit reached $69.2 billion. We saw strong demand across all of our product categories, with iPhone revenue particularly robust during the holiday season.

Services continues to be a growth engine. We now have over 1.2 billion paid subscriptions across our platform, and Services revenue reached new highs.

We invested $10.9 billion in research and development during the quarter, as we continue to push the boundaries of innovation across our product lines, including Apple Intelligence and our spatial computing platform.

Kevan Parekh, CFO:

Thank you, Tim. Let me walk through the financial details.

Total revenue of $143.8 billion, as Tim mentioned, was an all-time record. Our operating income was $50.9 billion, and net income came in at $42.1 billion. Diluted earnings per share were $2.84, also a new record.

EBITDA for the quarter was $54.2 billion. Our operating expenses, including R&D and SG&A, totaled $18.4 billion.

Turning to cash flow, we generated $53.9 billion in operating cash flow and $51.6 billion in free cash flow. Capital expenditures were $2.4 billion.

During the quarter, we returned approximately $28.6 billion to shareholders, including $24.7 billion in share repurchases and $3.9 billion in dividends.

Looking at the balance sheet, we ended the quarter with $45.3 billion in cash and cash equivalents. Total assets were $379.3 billion and total debt was $90.5 billion. Total stockholders' equity was $88.2 billion.

Analyst Q&A:

Analyst: Can you comment on the gross margin trajectory? It seems to have improved from last year.

Tim Cook: Yes, our gross margin of 48.2% improved from last year's Q1 level of 46.9%. This reflects a combination of favorable product mix, the growing contribution of Services, and our continued operational efficiency.

Analyst: How should we think about operating expenses going forward?

Kevan Parekh: Operating expenses for the quarter were $18.4 billion. We continue to invest heavily in R&D at $10.9 billion, which reflects our commitment to innovation. We expect operating expenses to remain in a similar range in the coming quarters.
